Max Fleischer

Animation pioneer & inventor of more than twenty motion picture production processes

Here's a time line of Max Fleischer's life:

  • 1883 - Born in Krak√≥w, Austrian Poland
  • 1887 - His family emigrated to the United States, settling in New York City
  • 1909 - Started working as a catalogue illustrator for the Crouse-Hinds Company
  • 1910 - Became art editor for Popular Science magazine
  • 1914 - Fleischer invents the rotoscope. An improvement in animation for tracing images from a live-action film
  • 1921 - Max and his brother Dave established Out of the Inkwell Films
  • 1926 - Produced My Old Kentucky Home which was the first cartoon to synchronize sound with animation
  • 1929 - Fleischer formed Fleischer Studios, Inc
  • 1930 - Max Fleischer's Betty Boop character was born out of a cameo caricature in the early Talkartoon, Dizzy Dishes
  • 1932 - The "Betty Boop" series began and became a big success for Fleischer
  • 1933 - Popeye the Sailor was introduced to audiences in the Betty Boop cartoon short, Popeye the Sailor
  • 1939 - Gulliver's Travels, an animated feature, was released in theaters and was the beginning of the financial difficulties of Fleischer Studios
  • 1940 - Max was relegated to business affairs and continued technical development. His efforts resulted in a reflex camera viewfinder and line transfer methods to replace the time-consuming and tedious process of cel inking
